CALL ON THE SENATE TO END WARRANTLESS BACKDOOR SEARCHES

The Senate is set to vote on NSA reform. Tell them to support the “backdoor searches amendment" to end warrantless searches of Americans’ communications.

This week, the Senate will vote on the USA Freedom Act, a bill meant to rein in NSA surveillance. However, a crucial reform is missing from the bill: A measure to close the backdoor search loophole that the NSA uses to search Americans’ communications without a warrant.

Under the FISA Amendments Act, the NSA collects the communications of foreigners believed to be overseas, without a warrant. However, the NSA can also sweep in large numbers of communications from Americans in contact with foreigners. In 2011, the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Court said that Americans' communications inadvertently swept up in this program could later be searched by the government without a warrant. This is in direct opposition to the Fourth Amendment’s warrant requirement.

A measure to close the backdoor search loophole has already passed the House with broad bipartisan support on a vote of 340-73. Now, Senators including Ron Wyden and Mark Udall are working on an improvement to the Senate bill to close the backdoor search loophole. It is crucial for the Fourth Amendment and privacy rights that this amendment be part of the final USA Freedom Act.

Call your Senators today and ask them to close the backdoor search loophole and end warrantless searches on Americans.
